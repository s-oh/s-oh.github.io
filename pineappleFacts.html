Interesting Facts

In some other parts of world, pineapples are called ananas.

Pineapples are native to Guadeloupe Island (where Christopher Columbus discovered them), Brazil, and Paraguay. Today, Costa Rica, the Philippines, and Brazil produce most of the world's pineapples.

A pineapple is made from mutliple fruitlets coming together, which makes a pineapple plant capable of only growing one pineapple at a time.

Pineapples can help ease arthritis pain because they contain the enzyme bromelain. Bromelain also breaks down protein, making pineapples good meat tenderizers.

Pineapples are also rich in vitamin C, which boosts your immune system.

The pineapple is the only bromeliad that produces edible fruit. This makes them the most economically significant plant in its family.

Pineapples take around 3 years to grow before they can be harvested. The first 28 months are for the maturation of the fruit and another 6 months are required in order for the pineapple to ripen.

Pineapples do not ripen much after they are harvested.
